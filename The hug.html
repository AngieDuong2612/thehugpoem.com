The Author: Angie Duong

This is a poem about the last hug from Angie's grandma

The Hug

The big hug in the airport,
The realization: it’s the last.
The cancer ’ll take grandma away,
The tears hold a silver lining.
The warm hug in the airport,
A scent of mint medicated oil.
The colour of tears on my shoulder,
The day that I became stronger.
The last hug in a video call,
My grandma with her face so thin.
Distance and time separate us,
two years’ long enough to break a heart…
The hug in my imagination,
My grandma is whole.
She sits beside me with a smile,
She stays here and never goes.
The hug will always be there…
